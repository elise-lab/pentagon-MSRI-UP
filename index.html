Manifold Modeling of Pentagon Spaces Using Laplacian Eigenfunctions

For a manifold \(\mathcal M\), define the infinite dimensional Hilbert space $$L^2(\mathcal M) := \{f : \mathcal M \rightarrow \mathbb{C} \mid f \text{ is square integrable}\}$$ with norm \(\langle u, v \rangle := \int_{\mathcal M} uv \,d\mu\). The Laplacian operator \(\Delta:L^2(\mathcal{M}) \rightarrow C^0(\mathcal{M})\) is the linear PDE taking \(f\mapsto \nabla^2 f\). Spectral Theorem: . The eigenfunctions of \(\Delta\) form an orthonormal basis for \(L^2(\mathcal{M})\); For any \(f \in L^2(\mathcal{M})\) there exists \(\hat{h}_d\in\mathbb{C}\) such that: $$\lim_{N \rightarrow \infty}\sum_{n=1}^N\hat{h}_d\cdot u_d = f$$ where \(u_d\) are the eigenfunctions of \(\Delta\).
